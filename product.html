Cork

Cork is a natural and renewable material that is also used as a filling material for synthetic turf pitches. It has good cushioning properties and is applied over a layer of sand. It is also odourless and is a fully biodegradable material obtained from the bark of the oak tree.

Cork is a lightweight, flexible, insulating and naturally water-repellent material. For these reasons, it is widely used in various industries. Cork is also used as a filling material for synthetic turf pitches. In this application, cork granules can be placed under the synthetic turf pitch to increase shock absorption and ensure player safety.

  • It is a lightweight material and can be used for different applications thanks to its flexibility.
  • It has naturally water-repellent properties. Therefore, it can reduce water absorption and increase its durability.
  • It has high abrasion and friction resistance.
  • It looks thick thanks to its flexible design.
  • It is an environmentally friendly material since it is obtained from a natural and renewable resource.
  • It provides good sound and heat insulation naturally.

How durable is the Cork filling material?

Cork is highly durable and naturally resistant to abrasion and friction. It can withstand high traffic and heavy sports activities while maintaining its structural integrity and cushioning properties for over 10-15 years, depending on maintenance.

Is Cork filling environmentally friendly?

Yes, absolutely. Cork is a 100% natural, renewable, and biodegradable material harvested from the bark of oak trees without cutting them down. Unlike synthetic fillings, it doesn't release microplastics into the environment, making it the most sustainable choice for artificial turf.

Does Cork require special maintenance?

Maintaining cork-filled turf is similar to standard SBR-filled fields. Regular brushing ensures an even distribution of granules. However, because cork is lightweight, it may require slightly more frequent top-ups after extreme weather events compared to heavier rubber infills.
